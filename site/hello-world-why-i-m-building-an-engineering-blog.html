Why start a blog in 2026?

In the world of technology, things move fast. We learn new tools, solve complex bugs, and optimize pipelines every single day. But if we don't document it, that knowledge often stays trapped in our local machines or becomes a blurry memory.

That’s why I created vmagueta.run.

The Goal

This is my corner of the internet to:

  • Document learnings: Turning "I figured it out" into a permanent resource.
  • Share knowledge: Helping other engineers who might be facing the same data or infrastructure challenges.
  • Build in public: Showing the process, not just the final result.

The Technical Choice: Why Marmite?

I wanted something as efficient as the code I try to write. No heavy databases, no complex setups. I chose Marmite because it’s a minimalist Static Site Generator (SSG) that lets me focus on what matters: the content. It takes simple Markdown files and, through a GitHub Actions workflow, deploys them straight to production. Simple, fast, and version-controlled.

What to expect

Moving forward, I’ll be sharing my journey through Data Engineering, Cloud Solutions, and the occasional technical hurdle I encounter along the way.

If you’re interested in technology, data, or just want to see how this blog evolves, stay tuned!

Feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn!
